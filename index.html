Menstruation

[mɛnstrʊˈeɪʃ(ə)n]

Noun

The regular discharge of blood and mucosal tissue (known as menses) from the inner lining of the uterus through the vagina. The first period usually begins between twelve and fifteen years of age, a point in time known as menarche.

Basic questions, answered right here.

Three questions that clear your confusion about supporting the cause.

Should it be normal ?

Menstruation is just another process the female human body has, similar to the other necessary life processes such as respiration and digestion. There's nothing that should be hidden about it, and we all know, the better the awareness, the lower the taboo.

Should you know this ?

Every person should be aware of this, so that the super-weird myths and superstitions can be discarded, menstruation becomes as normal as any other process of the human body, so that women won't have to face all types of restrictions they currently do.

Can something be done ?

There are many ways you can contribute, even if you aren't the gender that faces the restrictions. Some of the ways include spreading awareness in rural areas, contribute in NGO campaigns, or simply have an open and broad-minded opinion about this.

GOVERNMENT INITIATIVES

MHS

Menstrual Hygiene Scheme

The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare has introduced a scheme for promotion of menstrual hygiene among adolescent girls in the age group of 10-19 year in rural areas. The major objectives of the scheme include increasing awareness among adolescent girls on Menstrual Hygiene, increasing access to and use of high quality sanitary napkins to adolescent girls in rural areas, and ensuring safe disposal of Sanitary Napkins in an environmentally friendly manner.

MHP

Menstrual Hygiene Programme

In many areas, there is complete negligence of menstrual hygiene due to low awareness levels and lack of access to sanitary products. This programme for promotion of Menstrual Hygiene combines health education for adolescent girls, providing a regular supply of sanitary napkins and enabling other sanitation measures such as access to water and toilets in schools and in the community through convergence with other programmes.

MBB

Menstruation Benefit Bill

The Menstruation Benefit Bill sought to provide women working in public and private sectors two days of paid menstrual leave every month. However, in some states, the concept is already a reality. A study of official records shows that the Government Girls School in Ernakulam, Kerala had allowed students to take a period leave in 1912 itself! The school gave the provision to menstruating women to skip their exams temporarily.
