The Leaders

RutuChakra

RutuChakra is a youth-run organization working to achieve menstrual equity and dignity through action, awareness and advocacy.
They strive to empower menstruators of all ages and backgrounds through good menstrual hygiene practices by breaking the silence about menstruation, to spread awareness about menstrual hygiene, usage and disposal of menstrual products and to provide products to as many individuals as possible.
Their objectives are:

    • To promote better awareness amongst all genders to overcome embarrassment, cultural practices, taboos around this topic.
    • To reach out to as many individuals (from all backgrounds) as possible.
    • To put in place a system to ensure a regular supply of menstrual products to the organizations.
    • Ensure the access to information on this topic and from a reliable source (By Conducting Workshops).
    • Introduce facilities to hygienically dispose sanitary napkins.

Sacchi Saheli

Sachhi Saheli is an NGO with a mission to enable all the levels of our society to act collectively to 'let’ every woman lead a life on her terms. It aims to fight social taboos, break through stigmas to give voice to women and talk about everything that the society hesitates to discuss. Shock, shame and lack of awareness leads one fourth of girls in India to quit schools when they start menstruating. It is sheer humiliation that the world’s largest democracy that is moving towards modernity is facing such primitive and trivial social issues which are still unattended.

Sachhi Saheli is different from other organizations in the very approach of tackling these social problems; it intends to act on the causes rather than the symptoms. Women helpline numbers, SOS agencies, rehabilitation centers, all focus on the after-math of incidents of harassment that women face every day. Sachhi Saheli aims to attack the evil right where it is conceived, the society.

The Ammada Trust

The Ammada Trust runs a campaign, #GiveHer5, to help provide sanitary napkins to rural girls so that they don't miss their schools during periods. The #GiveHer5 campaign is attempting to give them 5 of 'those' days back, so they never miss out on school again.
The campaign was initiated by Ashok Kurien and the Ammada Trust to crowdsource funds to give girls Saafkins - a reusable, 12-hour menstrual protection," stated the website of GiveHer5 campaign. As a part of the campaign, the trust seeks donation starting from Rs 150 to help rural school girls to continue their studies during periods.

As a part of its initiative "Not Just a Piece of Cloth", Goonj provides clean cloth sanitary pads to women in villages.

World of Women

The Bengaluru-based WOW started its journey 3 years ago. It primarily focuses on distributing menstrual kits to poor women in the city and neighbouring areas.
Each WOW kit includes one year supply of quality sanitary napkins, soaps and two panties. Each kit costs Rs 500. For their work, WOW depends on financial aid from various donors. WOW members also conduct regular awareness programmes on menstrual health and hygiene.

Visit their website, here.

Jatan Sansthan

The Jatan Sansthan has the 'North-East project for Cloth-Pad production'.The project is a Menstrual Health Promotion and Livelihood Initiative.
Menstrual hygiene awareness progammes have been held in the 4 states where SHG groups associated with NERCORMP have been trained. A 15 member team were invited to Jatan Sansthan where they leaned production techniques and other details of how to manage and run a cloth pad production centre.
Additional components to the initiative was a survey conducted with 200 women on knowledge, attitude and practice around menstruation management, which guided the many components of the project.
